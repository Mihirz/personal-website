about

I'm a freshman at UC Berkeley studying EECS, mathematics, and neuroscience. I spend most of my time on research and building things — a habit picked up somewhere between competitive robotics and a late-night MIT OCW binge.

Right now I work with Chang Lab at UCSF on using speech as a biomarker for depression and chronic pain, and I'm co-building Working Memory, a small tool that helps you pick up where you left off in a coding session.

Outside of that: distance running, reading about consciousness, and anything brain–computer-interface shaped.

experience

ML Speech Analysis — DBS for Depression & Chronic Pain

Chang Lab · UCSF
  • Building speech as a robust biomarker for major depressive disorder and refractory chronic pain.
  • Predicting symptom severity from 15+ acoustic features with SVMs over linguistic content.
  • Using model outputs to close the loop on automated deep-brain-stimulation treatment.

Co-Founder — Working Memory startup

workingmemory.vip
